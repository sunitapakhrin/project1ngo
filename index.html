NEPAL COVID RELIEF

Nepal Covid Relief Response

Date: 14 May, 2021

A second wave of COVID-19 has left many hospitals without oxygen supplies or available beds. Hospitals and health centres across Nepal are struggling to cope with the sheer numbers of new cases. Patients in critical conditions are being turned away, many dying at home without access to medical help.

Since 25 of April 2021, Helping Hand has been mobilising a global response to the ongoing humanitarian crisis in Nepal. We have been working in partnership with NGOs, corporates and local and national Government to provide vital medical equipment such as oxygen concentrators, ventilators, tanks and broader medical equipment.

Helping Hand volunteers across the globe have been working tirelessly behind the scenes coordinating shipments of medical supplies. The donations will help towards frontline response to the pandemic. Together - we can protect more people from the worst effects of this outbreak and get medical supplies urgently to where they need to be.

Date: 27 April, 2021

We are currently witnessing a humanitarian crisis in Nepal of proportions never witnessed before as medical infrastructure is buckling under the pressure of the global pandemic. There have been at least 300,000 new infections every day in the past week, with cases continuing to rise at unprecedented levels.

We are immensely grateful to all those who have donated oxygen concentrators, of which the first batch of 200 are on its way with Virgin Atlantic and will be on the ground in Kathmandu Saturday 1st May 2021.

We are currently supporting medical networks across India, comprising of established medical organisations, facilities and NGO’s who are working on the ground to assist COVID-19 patients.

Your generous donations will go towards our vital medical programmes to support vulnerable communities and people across Nepal.

Relief measures by Helping Hand for COVID 19 distressed

  • 1. Providing Cooked Food & Ration for Needy
  • 2. Providing free of cost oxygen cylinders
  • 3. Providing Masks and medicine
  • 4. Providing Hydraulic beds to patients
  • 5. Sanitizing areas that are most vulnerable
